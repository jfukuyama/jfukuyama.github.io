Course Overview

This course aims to give students practice at working through and presenting the kinds of projects one might encounter as a statistical consultant. To that end, the bulk of the course will be devoted to presentations or discussion of a number of case studies. Each case study will be presented by either one student or a small group of students, but everyone is expected to discuss the methods employed by the presenting group and provide feedback to the presenters about what they did well and what they did poorly.

A rough schedule is as follows:

  • In weeks 1-3 we will read and discuss the first four chapters of the book.
  • Weeks 4-13 will be a combination of presentation/discussion of round-one (smaller) projects and in-class activities (subject to change, but tentatively on graphics, reproducibility, and writing).
  • Weeks 14-15 will be for final project presentations and discussion.

Textbook

The primary textbook will be Statistical Consulting, by Javier Cabrera and Andrew McDougall.

Additional readings will be posted on the course website.

Assessment

Final grades will be based on:

  • 10% reading responses
  • 25% participation (primarily peer feedback)
  • 25% round-one projects
  • 40% final projects

Reading responses

The first couple weeks of class we’ll read the book and discuss. You will be required to submit a short (<1pg) reading response for each reading.

Participation

The participation grade will be based on class attendance, participating in discussions, and providing written feedback on your classmates’ presentations.

Round-one projects

Every student will present at least one smaller-scale project. We will discuss whether to do these individually or as part of a group.

Final project

Final projects will be done in groups, with a target of 3 members per group. The deliverables for these projects will be:

  • A mock first-meeting writeup (due week 6)
  • A preliminary analysis writeup/presentation (done in weeks 8-12)
  • A final report (due on the last day of class)
  • A final presentation (in the last 1-2 weeks of class)

Options for the final project will be posted around week 4.
